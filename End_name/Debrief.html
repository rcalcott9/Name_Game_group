Debriefing: Coordination games and cooperative matching

1. What was this study about?

This study investigates how people coordinate their choices in cooperative matching games where partners must select the same letter to achieve successful coordination.

2. How was the study conducted?

All participants were recruited via Prolific. They played a 15-round letter coordination game where they tried to choose the same letter as their partner from a set of 10 possible letters (Q, M, X, Y, F, J, P, R, C, D). After each round, partnerships were randomly reshuffled within groups of 4 participants.

3. What was the hypothesis?

We predict that coordination success will depend on how players develop shared strategies for letter selection at the population level. We are interested in understanding how coordination emerges through repeated interactions across the participant population.

4. Did we tell you everything?

Yes, all aspects of the game worked exactly as described. We are studying coordination patterns at the population level to understand how shared strategies emerge through repeated interactions.

5. Why is this study important?

Understanding how people coordinate without explicit communication has important implications for teamwork, collaboration, and decision-making in various contexts where partners must work together to achieve shared goals. This research also helps us understand how information sharing affects cooperation.
